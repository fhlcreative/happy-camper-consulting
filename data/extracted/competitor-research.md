## Direct Competitor: The Sherry Group
**URL:** https://www.thesherrygroupllc.com/
**Industry:** Nonprofit growth / camp consulting (Iowa-based)

### What They Do Well
- Clear hero statement: "You lead a nonprofit, camp, or mission-driven organization. We help you plan and grow with purpose."
- Multiple prominent testimonials with real names and organizations
- Podcast ("Rest Hour Radio") — content marketing for camps
- Service cards with clear categories (Strategic Planning, Fundraising, Master Site Planning, Executive Coaching, Board Development)
- Social proof quotes: "Thoughtful, collaborative, insightful" — Camp Wood YMCA

### What They Do Poorly
- **Placeholder text still on site** — "Describe some quality or feature of the company" appears on service cards (unprofessional)
- Design is template-heavy, Squarespace feel
- No hero image — just text on background
- Service descriptions are thin

### Takeaways for HCC
- We MUST have testimonials — Sherry Group's testimonials are their strongest asset
- A podcast or content hub would differentiate (future phase)
- Even a mediocre competitor has more trust signals than HCC's current site

## Design Inspiration from Consulting Website Research

### Top Patterns from Best Consulting Websites (ConsultingSuccess.com analysis)

1. **Personal brand is everything** — David C. Baker, Jon Persson, Dana Mwangi all lead with their name/face. Solo consultants MUST be visible. Jeff needs a photo.

2. **Thought leadership content** drives organic traffic. Jon Persson gets 50%+ of revenue from SEO-driven blog content. HCC should plan for content.

3. **Transparent process** removes friction. Baker publishes prices, terms, and client criteria. HCC's Calendly approach is good but needs more context around process/pricing expectations.

4. **Niche positioning wins.** Adam Cooper dominates construction consulting by narrowing focus. HCC's camp niche is excellent — lean into it harder.

5. **Website as "storefront"** (Shannon Arvizu) — it validates you before the first call. HCC's current site does the opposite — it raises doubt.

## Visual Direction Recommendations

### Color Palette
- **Primary:** Deep forest green (#2d5016 or similar) — nature, growth, camp outdoors
- **Secondary:** Warm gold/amber (#d4a843) — campfire, warmth, trust
- **Neutral:** Soft cream/off-white (#faf8f2) — natural, warm paper tone
- **Text:** Deep charcoal (#1a1a1a)
- **Accent:** Muted terracotta or warm brown for variety

### Typography
- **Headings:** DM Serif Display or Playfair Display — authoritative, warm
- **Body:** DM Sans or Source Sans 3 — clean, readable
- **Accent/Pull quotes:** Italic serif for testimonials and callouts

### Photography Style
- Outdoor/nature settings — forests, lakes, campfire, cabins
- Leadership/team moments — people in conversation, working together
- Warm natural lighting — golden hour, dappled sunlight
- NOT stock-looking corporate boardroom shots
- Real camp environments preferred if available

### Layout Inspiration
- Split-layout hero (text left, image right) with warm overlay
- Card grid for the 4 HCOM™ layers (icon + heading + description)
- Full-width testimonial band with warm background
- Alternating content sections (left-image/right-text, then swap)
- Generous white space with intentional rhythm
- Bold stat counters: "30+ Years", "X Camps Served", etc.
