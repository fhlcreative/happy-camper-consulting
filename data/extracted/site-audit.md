# Happy Camper Consulting — Site Audit

**Date:** 2026-02-17
**URL:** https://happycamperconsultant.com/

---

## First Impression (5-Second Test)
**Grade: D+**
A stranger landing on this site would see walls of text with no hero image, no visual hierarchy, and no immediate sense of what this business does. The logo is the only visual element. It takes significant reading before you understand this is camp consulting. No photo of Jeff, no camp imagery, no visual storytelling at all.

## Visual Design
**Grade: D**
- **Outdated/generic:** The site appears to be built on a basic CMS template (possibly Cargo, Hugo, or similar static generator) with minimal customization
- **No hero section:** No full-width imagery, no background photos, no visual impact above the fold
- **No images anywhere:** The entire site is text-only (aside from the logo). No camp photos, no team photos, no icons, no visual elements
- **Typography:** Adequate but uninspiring. No display fonts, no typographic personality
- **Color:** Minimal — the logo has green tones but the site doesn't carry a strong color palette
- **White space:** Excessive — content feels sparse and disconnected rather than intentionally spacious
- **No visual hierarchy:** Every section looks the same — H2 + paragraph, repeated

## Mobile Experience
**Grade: C**
- Site is likely responsive (simple text layout) but there's nothing to evaluate — no complex layouts, no images, no interactive elements
- No sticky CTA bar
- No click-to-call (no phone number exists)
- The single "Start a Conversation" button is present

## Content Quality
**Grade: B-**
- **Strong copy quality:** The writing is actually quite good — authoritative, professional, clear
- **Good framework articulation:** The Healthy Camp Operating Model™ and VDMS are well-explained
- **Repetitive:** The same messages (alignment, governance, culture) are repeated across pages without enough variation
- **Missing content:** No case studies, no testimonials, no about/bio page, no results/outcomes data
- **No Jeff:** The consultant's name barely appears. No photo, no bio, no credentials listed, no personal story

## Trust Signals
**Grade: F**
- **Zero testimonials** on the site (competitor The Sherry Group has prominent testimonials)
- **No certifications or credentials** displayed
- **No client logos** or partnership badges
- **No "30+ years" prominently displayed** — it's buried in body copy
- **No photo of the consultant** — feels anonymous
- **No social proof whatsoever** — a visitor has no reason to trust this person

## Conversion Path
**Grade: D**
- **No phone number anywhere** on the site
- **No email address** anywhere on the site
- **Single CTA:** "Start a Conversation" → Calendly. That's the only conversion path
- **No contact form** — just a Calendly link
- **No urgency or specificity** in CTA language
- Calendly link requires a pre-questionnaire which adds friction

## SEO Basics
**Grade: D-**
- **Title tags:** "Happy Camper Consulting Home Page" — not optimized, includes "Home Page" literally
- **Meta descriptions:** Present but not optimized for keywords + CTA
- **H1 structure:** Unclear — appears to use H2 for all section headers
- **No alt text** (no images to have alt text on)
- **No sitemap.xml** likely
- **No schema/structured data** likely
- **No breadcrumbs**
- **Subpage titles broken:** VDMS and Conversation pages both show "S.C.M.A. Impact & Outcome Data" as their title — wrong page, wrong content association

## Performance
**Grade: B+**
- Likely very fast (almost no assets to load — just text and one logo image)
- But "fast because empty" isn't a win

## Local SEO
**Grade: F**
- **No city/state mentioned** — "nationwide" service but no geographic anchoring
- **No NAP** (Name, Address, Phone) — literally missing all three components
- **No Google Business Profile** linkage
- **No service area** defined
- No geo meta tags

## AI Readiness
**Grade: D**
- Content is extractable text (good)
- No structured data/schema
- No FAQ sections with proper markup
- No llms.txt
- No answer capsule patterns
- Content could be reorganized to be much more AI-friendly

---

## Summary of Critical Issues

1. **Completely invisible consultant** — Jeff has no bio, no photo, no credentials on site
2. **Zero trust signals** — no testimonials, no client logos, no credentials
3. **No contact information** — no phone, no email, just a Calendly link
4. **No images at all** — the site is 100% text, feels unfinished
5. **Broken page titles** — VDMS and Conversation pages share wrong titles
6. **No SEO foundation** — missing schema, proper titles, geographic targeting
7. **No visual design** — looks like a wireframe, not a finished website
8. **Repetitive content flow** — every page follows same monotonous H2 + paragraph pattern
