You can have the best programming, the newest facilities, and a killer marketing plan — but if your culture is broken, none of it matters.

I've seen it over and over in 32 years of camp leadership: the camps that thrive aren't the ones with the biggest budgets. They're the ones where staff genuinely care about each other, where values aren't just on a poster in the dining hall, and where leadership walks the talk.

## The Culture Problem Nobody Talks About

Most camp directors I work with know something feels off. Retention is down. Staff morale is shaky. Parents are harder to please. But when I ask about their culture, they point to their mission statement.

A mission statement isn't culture. Culture is what happens when nobody's watching.

![Camp counselors outdoors](/images/articles/camp-team.jpg)

## Three Signs Your Camp Culture Needs Work

1. **Staff turnover is climbing.** If your best counselors aren't coming back, it's not about the pay. It's about how they felt.

2. **Communication is top-down only.** When staff don't feel safe bringing concerns up, problems fester until they explode.

3. **You're reacting instead of leading.** If every week feels like crisis management, your systems aren't supporting your people.

## Where to Start

Culture change doesn't happen overnight, and it doesn't happen through a single training. It happens through consistent, intentional leadership at every level.
